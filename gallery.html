Objective 2

Work in a creative leadership role on a minimum of one (1) complete digital project produced in the Production Studio courses. Creative leadership roles include director, writer, producer, editor, cinematographer, sound designer, visual effects supervisor, compositing supervisor, art director, production designer, or graphic designer.

Ethereal Sound Scape

This is an ethereal soundscape I created for an unannounced game in development during Production Studio. The task was to create a calming but also slightly uneasy sound that would be played in a certain area of this game. I created and recorded all the sounds, then mixed them all together.

Facility Sound Scape

This is a facility soundscape I created for an unannounced game in development during Production Studio. The task was to create an eerie aural environment with various sounds looped in to make it feel as if something is with you. I created and recorded all the sounds, then mixed them all together.

Objective 3

Create visuals that exhibit professional quality in terms of camerawork, composition, and lighting.

Conceptual Shooting

This was assignment 3.1 from DVA101, where we were tasked with conveying a message through only visuals. No sound was allowed, and we were only supposed to rely on lighting, composition, and camerawork. I conveyed the message of commitment and focus by having the actor finish a puzzle. The professor enjoyed my assignment and showed it to the class as an example.

Anti-Littering PSA

This was the final assignment for DVA101, where we were to create a 30-second advertisement or a 30-second Public Service Announcement. I decided to go with an anti-littering PSA, where the message was that you should pick up litter even if it's not yours. The professor thought I did a great job and asked for my permission to air it on a sports broadcast he was in charge of.

Objective 4

Integrate composite shots into complete works by employing industry-standard tools and techniques.

Digital Compositing

This is a personal exercise I did using Nuke. I took CGI assets and composited them into one scene. The assets I used were Earth, Earth lights, Earth atmosphere, Earth clouds, a ship, stars, and a lens flare. I took all of them and made a seamless scene.

Title Sequence

This was my final assignment for DVA254. The task was to create a minimum 30-second-long title sequence for a made-up film. I decided to make my title sequence for a comedy film where an estranged father and son go on a camping trip and everything seems to go horribly wrong. Given the made-up film would be a comedy, I figured a fun title sequence would be in order, so I decided to animate a flipbook-style sequence. I took several assets and composited them together, then animated them in After Effects. The professor loved my submission and said it was the "best student title sequence" he'd ever seen.

Objective 5

Edit and color grade digital projects using industry-standard nonlinear editors and post-production tools.

Morning Routine

The Spa Edit

This was assignment 5.1 from DVA101, where we were provided the raw footage from a short film, and we were supposed to edit the film together following along with the script. This process involved editing, sound design, color correction, and even some compositing.

Objective 6

Record, edit, and mix high-quality audio, including dialogue, sound effects, and music, which are integrated into a complete digital production.

Alien SoundScape

This was assignment 5.3 from Digital Fundamentals. The goal was to create an aural environment for an alien world. I recorded all the sounds myself and mixed them together to create an alien planet with a stormy atmosphere and a creature in the distance.

Audio Story

This was assignment 5.4 from Digital Fundamentals. The goal was to tell an immersive story using sound to make the listener feel in the story world. The sounds used in this one were downloaded from the internet, but I edited and mixed the sounds together to create an immersive experience for the listener.
